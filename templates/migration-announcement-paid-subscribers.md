# Migration Announcement Email — Paid Subscribers

Send this to paid subscribers **separately** from the general announcement, ideally a few days earlier. Paid subscribers have money on the line; they should never learn about a migration from a generic email. Read [Migrating Paid Subscribers from Substack](../guides/migrate-paid-subscribers.md) before sending — the promises in this email (no double billing, no interrupted access) must be backed by your actual plan.

Related: [free subscriber version](migration-announcement-free-subscribers.md) · [full checklist](../checklist.md#6-handle-paid-subscribers)

---

**Subject:**

```txt
Important update about your subscription
```

**Body:**

```md
Hi {{first_name}},

I’m moving this newsletter from Substack to a new publishing setup.

Because you are a paid subscriber, I want to be extra clear: I’m handling the migration carefully so your access is not interrupted.

For now, you do not need to do anything. I’ll send another update if any action is required from you, especially if the new setup requires confirming your subscription again.

You will not be intentionally charged twice, and I’ll make sure paid access is handled fairly during the transition.

Thanks again for supporting the newsletter.

{{sender_name}}
```

---

## Tips

- Only send this once you have actually decided how billing will transition ([transfer vs re-subscribe](../guides/migrate-paid-subscribers.md#the-core-decision-transfer-billing-or-re-subscribe)). If readers ask follow-up questions, you need real answers.
- If readers **will** need to re-subscribe, say so in this email instead of promising a later update — give exact steps, a generous deadline, and what happens to their remaining paid time.
- Add a line naming the address they can write to with billing questions, and answer those emails fast during the transition.
- Consider adding a specific date: "Your access on the current setup continues at least through {{date}}."
